The tech world was abuzz with rumors of OpenAI's foray into hardware, and now the company has officially unveiled its first branded device: the Codex Micro. This $230 mini keyboard is designed specifically for power users of OpenAI's Codex AI platform, offering a unique way to monitor and interact with multiple AI agents at a glance.

<Callout type="takeaway">The Codex Micro is OpenAI's first foray into branded hardware, designed to streamline interactions with multiple AI agents.</Callout>

## What happened
The Codex Micro is a collaboration with Work Louder, a company known for its customizable keyboards. This device features a compact design with six frosted keys that provide color-coded feedback on up to six Codex threads. These keys can cycle through different colors to indicate the status of each thread, such as idle, thinking, complete, or error. The keyboard also includes six programmable buttons for common Codex tasks, which can be remapped through software.

## Why it matters
The Codex Micro is significant because it represents OpenAI's expansion into hardware, specifically targeting power users of its Codex platform. This device can be particularly useful for users who manage multiple AI agents simultaneously, providing a quick and easy way to monitor and interact with these agents. The keyboard's design and functionality are tailored to enhance productivity and streamline workflow for Codex users.

When considering the Codex Micro, think about your workflow and how you interact with AI agents.
