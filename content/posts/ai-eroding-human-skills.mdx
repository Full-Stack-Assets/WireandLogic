The increasing reliance on artificial-intelligence tools is sparking concerns about the potential erosion of human skills. A recent study found that physicians' ability to spot pre-cancerous growths during colonoscopies declined after they had grown accustomed to using an AI tool to help with the task. This phenomenon, known as 'deskilling,' is a growing concern for medical specialists, computer scientists, and other workers. Seventy per cent of nurses and 77% of physicians are worried about losing their skills due to over-reliance on AI systems.

   <Callout type="takeaway">The use of AI tools can lead to a decline in human skills, even in highly trained professionals.</Callout>

   ## What happened
   Researchers have been investigating the impact of AI tools on human skills in various fields, including medicine and computer science. A study of physicians in Poland who specialize in endoscopy found that their performance dropped significantly whenever an AI system was unavailable. The study suggests that continuous exposure to AI tools can cause clinicians to become less motivated, less focused, and less responsible when making cognitive decisions without AI assistance.

   Another study found that software engineers who rely heavily on AI tools may experience a decline in their own skills and abilities. This is because AI tools can handle routine tasks, but they can also lead to a lack of practice and a decline in human skills.

   The use of AI tools is becoming increasingly common in various industries, and it is essential to understand the potential risks and benefits of relying on these tools.

   ## Why it matters
   The erosion of human skills due to AI tools has significant implications for various industries, including medicine and technology. If professionals rely too heavily on AI tools, they may lose the skills and abilities that are essential for their work. This can lead to a decline in the quality of care or services provided, which can have serious consequences.

   ## How to think about it
   To mitigate the risks of AI tools eroding human skills, it is essential to strike a balance between using AI tools and maintaining human skills and abilities. Professionals should be aware of the potential risks and benefits of relying on AI tools and take steps to ensure that they are using these tools in a way that complements their human skills, rather than replacing them.
