Norway has taken a significant step in regulating the use of artificial intelligence in its education system. The country's government has announced a near ban on the use of generative AI tools by elementary school pupils, citing concerns over the potential negative impact on learning. This move is part of a broader effort to address declining education test scores and ensure that students develop essential skills. <Callout type="takeaway">Norway's ban on AI in elementary schools highlights the need for a balanced approach to technology integration in education.</Callout> ## What happened Norway's Prime Minister Jonas Gahr Stoere announced the new policy, which restricts the use of AI tools in elementary schools and limits their use in older children's education to prevent a negative impact on learning. The government has also proposed legislation to fund the use of more books in classrooms, reversing the trend towards computer tablets. The ban is set to be imposed from the new school year beginning in late August. Pupils from first through seventh grade, aged 6 to 13, should not use AI, while those in lower secondary school, aged 14 to 16, can use tools under teachers' supervision. In upper secondary education, from ages 17 to 19, students should learn to use AI appropriately. The Norwegian government has also announced plans to ban children from using social media until they turn 16. ## Why it matters The ban on AI in elementary schools is significant, as it acknowledges the potential risks associated with over-reliance on technology in education. By restricting AI use, the government aims to ensure that students develop essential skills, such as reading, writing, and mathematics.
