The United States has been experiencing a puzzling phenomenon - despite being one of the wealthiest countries in the world, its citizens are reporting low levels of happiness. This trend has been observed in various surveys, including the General Social Survey, which showed a sharp decline in self-reported happiness after COVID. The Federal Reserve's measure of US worker satisfaction has also fallen to its lowest level since 2014, and consumer sentiment has reached an all-time low. These findings are intriguing, given that the US economy has been performing well, with low unemployment rates and significant growth. <Callout type="takeaway">The disconnect between America's economic prosperity and its citizens' happiness is a complex issue that warrants further exploration.</Callout> ## What happened The decline in self-reported happiness in the US population has been documented by various researchers, including Sam Peltzman, who analyzed data from the General Social Survey. His findings indicate a sudden and sharp decline in happiness after COVID, which has persisted through 2024. This trend is not limited to specific demographics, but rather is an across-the-board decline experienced by practically every group. The World Happiness Report has also ranked the US at its lowest level ever, largely due to the decline in well-being among young people. ## Why it matters The implications of this trend are significant, as feelings of happiness and satisfaction can shape consumer behavior, political attitudes, and ultimately, economic policies. The gap between "hard data" and "soft data" is widening, and it is essential to consider both aspects to understand the future of the US economy. The Tragic Twenties, as this decade has been dubbed, are characterized by low levels of happiness and satisfaction, despite economic growth. <ProsCons> <Pros> <li>The US economy has been performing well, with low unemployment rates and significant growth.</li> <li>More Americans are breaking into the upper middle class, and workers at the bottom of the income distribution have seen their wages grow faster than those at the top.</li> <li>The US has a strong tradition of entrepreneurship and innovation, which has driven economic growth and prosperity.</li> </Pros> <Cons> <li>The decline in self-reported happiness and satisfaction is a concerning trend that warrants attention.</li> <li>The gap between economic growth and happiness is widening, indicating a potential disconnect between economic policies and citizens' well-being.</li> <li>The US has fallen to its lowest ranking ever in the World Happiness Report, which may have implications for its global reputation and attractiveness.</li> </Cons> </ProsCons> ## How to think about it To address this issue, it is essential to consider the complexities of human happiness and satisfaction. Economic growth and prosperity are important factors, but they are not the only determinants of well-being. Other aspects, such as social connections, health, and a sense of purpose, also play a crucial role.
