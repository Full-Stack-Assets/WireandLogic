Companies are struggling to implement AI effectively due to a lack of self-awareness and clear goals. This issue is not a technical maturity problem, but rather a fundamental one. Many companies are haphazardly successful despite themselves, lacking a clear understanding of what they are trying to accomplish or how they are doing it. 
<Callout type="takeaway">The inability of companies to describe what they want is a major obstacle to effective AI implementation.</Callout>
## What happened
The problem lies in the fact that most companies are chaotic black boxes that barely work, with unclear and constantly-changing vision and goals. This makes it difficult for AI to be effective, as it relies on clear instructions and execution. Companies that know exactly what they want are thriving with AI, but this is a small percentage. 
Most companies are not self-aware and lack the ability to articulate themselves well enough to be ready for AI. This is evident in the fact that many companies are unable to describe what they are trying to accomplish or how they are doing it. 
The narrative that AI is not helping enough companies yet is misleading, as the problem lies not with AI, but with the companies themselves.

## Why it matters
The stakes are high, as companies that are not ready for AI are in grave danger of being left behind. The implications are significant, with potential consequences including decreased competitiveness and revenue. The people affected are not just the companies themselves, but also their employees, customers, and stakeholders. 
The lack of self-awareness and clear goals in companies is a major obstacle to effective AI implementation. This issue is not limited to small companies, but also affects large corporations. 
The ability of companies to articulate themselves well enough to be ready for AI is crucial, as it determines their ability to thrive in a rapidly changing business environment.

## How to think about it
To think about this issue, companies need to take a step back and assess their own self-awareness and ability to articulate themselves. This involves identifying their strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats, as well as their goals and vision. Companies need to be honest with themselves about their limitations and areas for improvement. 
By doing so, companies can develop a clear understanding of what they want to accomplish and how they can use AI to achieve their goals. This requires a fundamental shift in how companies operate, from being chaotic black boxes to being self-aware and goal-oriented. 
The key is to focus on developing a clear understanding of what the company wants to accomplish, and then using AI to execute and optimize those goals.

<Callout type="warning">Companies that are not self-aware and lack clear goals should not attempt to implement AI, as it can make things worse.</Callout>
