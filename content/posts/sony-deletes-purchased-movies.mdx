Sony has begun notifying PlayStation users that it will delete more than 550 digital movies and TV shows from their accounts due to an expired licensing agreement with Studio Canal. The affected titles, which include popular films like Terminator 2: Judgment Day, Hot Fuzz, and Paddington, will be removed from users' video libraries on September 1, 2026.

<Callout type="takeaway">Sony's move highlights the risks of digital ownership, where users may lose access to purchased content due to licensing agreements.</Callout>

## What happened
The deletion of purchased movies and TV shows is a result of Sony's licensing agreement with Studio Canal coming to an end. Sony has chosen not to renew the agreement, which means users will no longer be able to access the affected content. This is not the first time Sony has removed digital content from users' accounts; in December 2023, the company announced that it would remove Discovery content, only to reverse the decision weeks later.

## Why it matters
This incident raises concerns about the nature of digital ownership. When users purchase digital content, they often assume they have permanent access to it. However, licensing agreements can expire, and content can be removed, leaving users without access to their purchased libraries. This highlights the importance of understanding the terms and conditions of digital purchases and the potential risks involved.
