The recent trend of AI being forced upon society has led to a growing backlash, with many people questioning its impact on human agency and autonomy. This pushback is not just about the potential risks and biases of AI, but also about the fundamental changes it is bringing to the human experience. As AI becomes more prevalent, it is altering the way we make choices, exercise judgment, and interact with the world around us. 
   <Callout type="takeaway">The increasing use of AI is threatening human judgment and autonomy, and it's essential to consider the long-term implications of relying on machines to make decisions for us.</Callout>
   ## What happened
   The use of AI has become widespread, with applications in various fields such as education, healthcare, and finance. However, this has also led to concerns about the potential risks and biases of AI, as well as its impact on human agency and autonomy. For instance, the use of AI in hiring and firing decisions has raised questions about fairness and transparency. Similarly, the reliance on AI in law enforcement has sparked debates about racial bias and discrimination.
   The growing use of AI has also led to a shift in the way we make choices and exercise judgment. With AI-powered recommendation engines, we are increasingly relying on machines to curate our cultural experiences and make decisions for us. This has significant implications for human autonomy and the development of practical judgment.
   Furthermore, the use of AI in various fields has also led to a loss of traditional skills and ways of thinking. For example, the reliance on GPS systems has atrophied our native capacity to orient ourselves, while the use of AI-powered writing tools has changed the way we write and communicate.
   ## Why it matters
   The impact of AI on human agency and autonomy is a critical issue that needs to be addressed. As AI becomes more prevalent, it is essential to consider the long-term implications of relying on machines to make decisions for us. This includes the potential risks and biases of AI, as well as its impact on human judgment and autonomy.
   The use of AI also raises significant questions about the future of work and the role of humans in the economy. As machines become more capable of performing tasks that were previously done by humans, there is a growing concern about the potential for job displacement and unemployment.

   ## How to think about it
   When considering the use of AI, it's essential to think critically about its potential impact on human agency and autonomy. This includes evaluating the potential risks and biases of AI, as well as its potential benefits and advantages. It's also crucial to consider the long-term implications of relying on machines to make decisions for us and to think about how we can mitigate the negative effects of AI.
