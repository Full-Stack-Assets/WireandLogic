The rise of AI has led to a significant decline in self-help nonfiction book sales. According to recent data, adult nonfiction sales have decreased by 9% in the first quarter of 2026 compared to the same period in 2025. Self-help books have been particularly affected, with a 26.3% decline in sales. This trend is not limited to one author or book, as many bestselling self-help authors have seen a significant drop in sales. <Callout type="takeaway">The rise of AI has disrupted the self-help nonfiction book industry, with many authors seeing a significant decline in sales.</Callout> ## What happened The decline in self-help book sales can be attributed to the rise of AI-powered tools such as ChatGPT, which provide users with instant access to information and answers. Many self-help books are essentially lookup tables or decision trees, providing readers with step-by-step instructions on how to achieve a specific goal. With AI-powered tools, users can now access this information instantly, without the need to purchase a book. Additionally, the rise of online content such as blogs, podcasts, and YouTube videos has also contributed to the decline in book sales. ## Why it matters The decline in self-help book sales has significant implications for authors, publishers, and readers. Many authors rely on book sales as a source of income, and a decline in sales can have a significant impact on their livelihood. Publishers also rely on book sales to generate revenue, and a decline in sales can lead to a reduction in the number of books published. Readers are also affected, as they may no longer have access to the same level of high-quality content. <ProsCons> <Pros> <li>AI-powered tools provide instant access to information</li> <li>Online content provides a wide range of perspectives and opinions</li> <li>Readers have more options for accessing information</li> </Pros> <Cons> <li>Decline in book sales affects authors and publishers</li> <li>Readers may no longer have access to high-quality content</li> <li>AI-powered tools may not provide the same level of depth and analysis as books</li> </Cons> </ProsCons> ## How to think about it The rise of AI and online content requires authors, publishers, and readers to rethink their approach to self-help nonfiction books. Authors need to consider how they can create content that is more engaging and valuable than what is available online. Publishers need to consider how they can adapt to the changing market and find new ways to generate revenue. Readers need to be aware of the potential limitations of AI-powered tools and online content, and seek out high-quality sources of information. <Callout type="warning">While AI-powered tools and online content provide many benefits, they also have limitations and potential drawbacks that readers should be aware of.</Callout> ## FAQ <FAQ> <Question q="What is causing the decline in self-help book sales?">The rise of AI-powered tools such as ChatGPT, which provide users with instant access to information and answers, is a significant factor in the decline of self-help book sales.</Question> <Question q="How are authors and publishers affected by the decline in book sales?">The decline in book sales can have a significant impact on authors and publishers, as they rely on book sales to generate revenue.</Question> <Question q="What can readers do to access high-quality self-help content?">Readers can seek out high-quality sources of information, such as books and online content from reputable authors and publishers, and be aware of the potential limitations of AI-powered tools and online content.</Question> </FAQ>
