The Swiss parliament has lifted a ban on the construction of new nuclear power plants, following a marathon debate. This decision allows for the possibility of building new nuclear power stations in Switzerland, with the final decision resting with the people. The National Council approved a counterproposal to the Blackout Initiative, which had aimed to prevent the construction of new nuclear power plants.

<Callout type="takeaway">The Swiss parliament's decision to lift the ban on new nuclear power plants marks a significant shift in the country's energy policy.</Callout>

## What happened
The construction of new nuclear power plants is set to become possible again in Switzerland. Following the Federal Council and the Council of States, the National Council now shares this view. On Thursday, it approved the counterproposal to the Blackout Initiative. The final decision will rest with the people. By a vote of 100 to 98, the National Council rejected a motion to refer the matter back to the Federal Council for clarification of financing issues.

The Swiss energy minister emphasized the need to keep the nuclear option open to guarantee the country's long-term energy supply. This was supported by right and centre parties but not by left-leaning parties. The Greens have already announced that they will call for a referendum.

The decision was made after a marathon debate, with various parties presenting their views on the matter. The National Council's decision is seen as a significant step towards determining the future of nuclear power in Switzerland.

## Why it matters
The decision to lift the ban on new nuclear power plants has significant implications for Switzerland's energy policy. The country has been grappling with the issue of how to ensure a stable and secure energy supply, and the construction of new nuclear power plants is seen as one possible solution. However, the decision is also expected to face opposition from environmental groups and other stakeholders who are concerned about the safety and environmental impact of nuclear power.
