## What happened
The 'cold blob' is a large patch of water in the North Atlantic, south of Greenland and Iceland, that has cooled by nearly 1 degree Celsius since 1900. Researchers have long debated the cause of this anomaly, with some attributing it to changes in atmospheric conditions and others to the weakening of the AMOC. A new study has found that the cooling of the 'cold blob' is not just a surface phenomenon, but also extends deep into the ocean, suggesting that it is indeed linked to the AMOC.

The AMOC is a vital component of the global ocean circulation system, responsible for transporting heat and nutrients across the Atlantic. Its weakening could have far-reaching consequences, including accelerated sea level rise on the US East Coast, extreme droughts in Africa, and shifts in the monsoon patterns in Asia.
