Midjourney, a company known for its AI image generation capabilities, has launched a medical division with a whole-body ultrasound scanner. The scanner, called the Midjourney Scanner, uses a ring of sensors to capture vertical slices of the inside of the body, looking at the composition of muscle, fat, bone, and organs. This technology has the potential to revolutionize the field of medicine by providing quick and accurate scans.

<Callout type="takeaway"> Midjourney's medical division aims to make preventative scanning accessible to billions through its magical spa experience. </Callout>

## What happened
Midjourney's CEO, David Holz, revealed the company's first hardware product, the Midjourney Scanner, which is an ultrasound-based full-body scanner. The scanner was developed in partnership with ultrasound tech company Butterfly Network. The scanning process starts with stepping onto a platform that drops down into the water on rails through a ring of thousands of transducers that create ultrasonic waves and then record the ripples from them passing through the body to analyze them and create detailed 3D images.

The Midjourney Scanner has the potential to provide MRI-grade imaging of the entire body in about 60 seconds. The company plans to launch a Midjourney Spa location in San Francisco's Union Square, which will feature 10 of the scanners. The spa will also have a gym, saunas, and cold plunges.

Midjourney's medical division is initially focusing on body composition maps, which do not require FDA clearance. However, the company plans to submit test results to the FDA over time for increased capabilities.
