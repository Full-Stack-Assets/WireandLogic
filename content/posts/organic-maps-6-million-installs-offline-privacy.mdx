Organic Maps, the open-source, privacy-focused offline maps and GPS application, recently announced it has reached over 6 million installs. Developed by the original creators of MapsWithMe (now MAPS.ME) and powered by OpenStreetMap data, the app distinguishes itself by offering a complete navigation experience without ads, tracking, or excessive permissions. This milestone highlights a growing user demand for digital tools that prioritize user privacy and functionality in environments without internet access.

<Callout type="takeaway">
Organic Maps demonstrates a significant user preference for privacy-centric, offline-capable navigation tools built on open-source principles.
</Callout>

## What happened
Organic Maps has achieved over 6 million installs across Android and iOS platforms, positioning itself as a leading alternative to mainstream mapping services. The app provides comprehensive offline capabilities, allowing users to download maps for entire countries or regions and utilize features like turn-by-turn navigation, search, and route planning without an active internet connection. It caters specifically to hikers, cyclists, and drivers with detailed cycling routes, hiking trails, contour lines, and elevation profiles.

The project is entirely open-source, licensed under Apache License 2.0, and is developed by a community committed to user privacy. The application has been verified by privacy projects like Exodus Privacy and TrackerControl for its lack of trackers and minimal permission requests. This commitment to user data protection, combined with its robust offline functionality, has resonated with a global user base seeking reliable navigation free from surveillance.

## Why it matters
In an era where personal data is frequently collected and monetized, Organic Maps offers a compelling counter-narrative by proving that a feature-rich application can thrive without compromising user privacy. Its success underscores a shift in user expectations, particularly among developers and builders who value open-source solutions and data sovereignty. For travelers, outdoor enthusiasts, and anyone operating in areas with unreliable internet, the app provides an indispensable tool that ensures continuous access to critical navigational information.

Furthermore, Organic Maps' reliance on OpenStreetMap data empowers a collaborative, community-driven approach to mapping. This model often results in more detailed and up-to-date information for niche areas, trails, and points of interest that might be overlooked by commercial mapping services. Its growth signals a potential future where privacy-respecting, community-supported tools gain significant traction, challenging the dominance of data-extractive platforms.

## How to think about it
For developers and builders, Organic Maps represents a strong example of how open-source principles and a commitment to user privacy can create a highly valuable product. When considering mapping solutions for personal use or integration into projects, it's crucial to weigh the trade-offs between the vast feature sets of commercial platforms and the ethical, privacy-preserving nature of alternatives like Organic Maps. If your use case prioritizes data security, offline reliability, or contributing to an open ecosystem, Organic Maps offers a robust and principled choice. Consider supporting such projects through donations or contributions to ensure their continued viability and evolution. It's a testament to the power of community-driven development to offer a viable, ethical alternative in a critical digital domain.

<Callout type="warning">
Users should be aware that Organic Maps, as a donation-funded project, faces ongoing financial sustainability challenges. This may impact the pace of new feature development and the long-term availability of resources needed to maintain and scale the application.
</Callout>

## FAQ
<FAQ>
  <Question q="How does Organic Maps ensure my privacy?">
    Organic Maps ensures privacy by operating without ads, trackers, or excessive permissions. It has been verified by independent privacy projects like Exodus Privacy and TrackerControl for its commitment to not collecting or sharing user data.
  </Question>
  <Question q="Can I use Organic Maps completely offline?">
    Yes, Organic Maps is designed for 100% offline functionality. After downloading the necessary map data for your desired regions, all features, including search, routing, and turn-by-turn navigation, work without an active internet connection.
  </Question>
  <Question q="What data does Organic Maps use for its maps?">
    Organic Maps is powered by OpenStreetMap (OSM) data. OSM is a collaborative, crowd-sourced project that provides highly detailed and frequently updated geographical data, often including points of interest and trails not found on proprietary maps.
  </Question>
