The t.me domain, a critical link for the messaging app Telegram, has been suspended. This domain is used for short links within the app, such as user profiles and channels. The suspension has caused outages for Telegram users.

<Callout type="takeaway">The suspension of t.me highlights the risks of relying on domains managed by organizations in certain countries.</Callout>

## What happened
The t.me domain, which is the country code top-level domain (ccTLD) for Montenegro, has been placed in a Server Hold status by the registry. This status prevents the domain from resolving, effectively suspending it. The .me domain is managed by an organization in Montenegro, despite being commercialized for its generic meaning.

The suspension was first reported on Hacker News and later confirmed by Domain Name Wire. According to Whois records, the domain is currently in a Server Hold status.

## Why it matters
The suspension of t.me has significant implications for Telegram and its users. As a critical domain for the app, its suspension causes widespread outages and disruptions. This incident highlights the risks associated with relying on domains managed by organizations in certain countries or regions.

The stability and resilience of domains managed by such organizations can be a concern. As one commenter on Hacker News noted, it is "class-A stupid" for critical parts of the internet to depend on "micronations" that may prioritize short-term gains over long-term stability.
