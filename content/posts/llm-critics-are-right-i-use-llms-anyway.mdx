Many critics of Large Language Models (LLMs) raise valid concerns about their limitations and potential drawbacks. However, despite these criticisms, LLMs have become an integral part of many developers' workflows.

    <Callout type="takeaway">The author, like many others, continues to use LLMs despite acknowledging the critics' concerns.</Callout>

    ## What happened
    The increasing prevalence of LLMs in software development has led to a growing dissonance between critics and users. At a recent conference, Armin Ronacher, a well-known software engineer, discussed the challenges of dealing with the flood of PRs generated by LLMs. His company, Earendil, has implemented measures to manage these PRs, but the issue persists.

    The use of LLMs has become widespread, with many developers relying on them for tasks such as coding and writing. However, this has also led to concerns about the potential atrophy of software engineering skills and the reliability of LLMs.

    ## Why it matters
    The debate surrounding LLMs is not just about their technical capabilities, but also about their impact on the software development community. As LLMs become more prevalent, it is essential to consider their implications on productivity, skill development, and the overall quality of software.

    The stakes are high, and the implications are far-reaching. If LLMs are not used judiciously, they could lead to a decline in software engineering skills and a homogenization of code.

      <Pros>

    ## How to think about it
    When considering the use of LLMs, developers should weigh the benefits and drawbacks and use them judiciously. It is essential to maintain a balance between relying on LLMs and developing one's own skills.

    <Callout type="warning">Be cautious of over-relying on LLMs, as this can lead to a decline in software engineering skills and a lack of critical thinking.</Callout>
