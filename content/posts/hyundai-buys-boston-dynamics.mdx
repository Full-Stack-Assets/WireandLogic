Hyundai Motor Group's recent acquisition of the remaining stake in Boston Dynamics marks a significant milestone in the robotics industry. The deal, valued at $325 million, gives Hyundai full control of the company, allowing it to further integrate Boston Dynamics' technology into its manufacturing capabilities. This move is expected to accelerate the development of cutting-edge robotics, featuring advanced mobility, manipulation, and vision capabilities. 
   <Callout type="takeaway">Hyundai's acquisition of Boston Dynamics is a strategic move to establish itself as a leader in the robotics industry.</Callout>
   ## What happened
   Hyundai Motor Group acquired the remaining 9.65% stake in Boston Dynamics from SoftBank for $325 million. The deal follows Hyundai's initial acquisition of an 80% stake in Boston Dynamics in 2021, valuing the company at $1.1 billion. Boston Dynamics has been making significant strides in the development of humanoid robots, including the Atlas robot, which is expected to begin work at Hyundai's electric vehicle plant in Georgia by 2028.
   The acquisition is a result of Hyundai's efforts to expand its presence in the robotics industry and develop advanced technologies that enhance people's lives. Boston Dynamics has already launched sales of its first commercial robot, Spot, and has hundreds of robots operating in various industries.
   The deal is expected to allow Hyundai and Boston Dynamics to leverage each other's strengths in manufacturing, logistics, construction, and automation. Together, they will create a robotics value chain, from robot component manufacturing to smart logistics solutions.
