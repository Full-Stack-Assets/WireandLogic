Anthropic is set to require ID verification for certain capabilities starting July 8, as announced in an email to Claude users. The email states that the verification is for enhanced security and will be conducted through a third-party service called Persona. This move has sparked concerns among users, with some expressing worries about the potential impact on their privacy and the frequency of verification requests. 
The requirement applies to retail accounts, including Free, Pro, and Max plans, while business customers remain unaffected. 
<Callout type="takeaway"> Anthropic's ID verification requirement is a significant development in the company's efforts to enhance security and comply with regulations.</Callout>
## What happened
Anthropic's decision to require ID verification is a result of the increasing complexity of its AI capabilities, which can perform multi-step tasks and integrate with third-party apps. The company's updated privacy policy states that user data may flow between Anthropic and third-party applications, raising concerns about data security. 
The verification process involves uploading a government-issued ID and taking a real-time selfie, which will be used solely for identity verification and not for model training. 
Anthropic has previously used identity verification for specific scenarios, such as child protection and accounts from unsupported regions, but this new requirement is more explicit and has a broader scope.
## Why it matters
The ID verification requirement has significant implications for Anthropic users, particularly those who value their privacy and are concerned about the potential risks of sharing personal data. 
The move also reflects the growing scrutiny of AI companies and their data collection practices, with regulators and users increasingly demanding greater transparency and accountability. 
The requirement may also have implications for the broader AI industry, as companies navigate the complex landscape of data security, privacy, and regulation.
