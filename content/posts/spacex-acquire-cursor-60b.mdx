## What happened
SpaceX and Cursor have partnered to develop a next-generation AI, combining Cursor's product and distribution expertise with SpaceX's Colossus supercomputer. The deal includes an option for SpaceX to acquire Cursor for $60 billion later this year. This move is seen as a strategic expansion of SpaceX's presence in the AI market. The partnership will leverage the strengths of both companies to create a cutting-edge AI solution.

The deal has been in the works for some time, with Cursor's senior engineering leaders, Andrew Milich and Jason Ginsberg, leaving the company to join xAI last month. Additionally, xAI will begin renting computing power from its data centers to Cursor, with the coding startup using tens of thousands of xAI chips to train its latest AI model.

SpaceX's Colossus supercomputer, which has the equivalent compute power of a million Nvidia H100 chips, will play a key role in the partnership. The supercomputer will provide the necessary processing power to develop and train the next-generation AI.
