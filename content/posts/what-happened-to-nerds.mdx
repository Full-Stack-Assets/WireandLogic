The tech industry has undergone a significant transformation in recent years, with its leaders shifting from humble, brilliant, and eccentric individuals to attention-seeking, egomaniacal personalities. This change has led to a loss of trust and a reevaluation of the cultural idea of the technologist. The industry's leadership has discovered that trust can be liquidated and converted into attention, but at a great cost.

<Callout type="takeaway"> The tech industry's shift from nerds to attention-seekers has led to a loss of trust and a reevaluation of the cultural idea of the technologist.</Callout>

## What happened
The tech industry spent forty years accumulating trust, with leaders like Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak embodying the idea of the perfectionist jerk and the gentle obsessive. However, over the last decade, the industry's leadership has changed, with many leaders now prioritizing attention and self-promotion over their work. This shift has been driven by the discovery that trust can be liquidated and converted into attention, which has become a valuable asset.

The transition from 'helpful, obsessive nerd' to 'tech oligarch' can be simplified into three phases. The first phase, from the late 1970s to 2007, saw the rise of leaders like Jobs and Wozniak, who were focused on their work and not on seeking attention. The second phase, from 2007 to the present, has seen the rise of leaders who prioritize attention and self-promotion.

## Why it matters
The shift from nerds to attention-seekers has significant implications for the tech industry and society as a whole. It has led to a loss of trust and a reevaluation of the cultural idea of the technologist. The industry's leadership must now consider the consequences of prioritizing attention over their work and the impact this has on their relationships with customers, employees, and the wider public.
